Did you know? Kung Pao Chicken is a famous Sichuan dish known for its spicy and sweet flavors.

Kung Pao Chicken

30 mins Medium
Main Course Spicy Stir-fry

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken (cubed)
  • 1/2 cup peanuts
  • 3 dried red chilies
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch

Instructions

  1. Marinate chicken with soy sauce and cornstarch.
  2. Stir-fry chicken with chilies and peanuts.
  3. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Did you know? Mapo Tofu is a classic Sichuan dish known for its numbing and spicy flavors.

Mapo Tofu

25 mins Easy
Main Course Vegetarian Spicy

Ingredients

  • 400g tofu (cubed)
  • 200g minced pork
  • 2 tbsp chili bean paste
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp Sichuan peppercorns

Instructions

  1. Stir-fry pork with chili bean paste.
  2. Add broth and tofu, simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Garnish with Sichuan peppercorns and serve.

Did you know? Peking Duck is a famous dish from Beijing, traditionally served with thin pancakes.

Peking Duck

2 hours Hard
Main Course Festive Special Occasion

Ingredients

  • 1 whole duck
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • Chinese pancakes
  • Green onions and hoisin sauce

Instructions

  1. Marinate duck with honey and soy sauce.
  2. Roast at 200°C until crispy.
  3. Serve in pancakes with hoisin sauce.

Did you know? Fried rice is a great way to use leftover rice and create a delicious meal.

Chinese Fried Rice

20 mins Easy
Main Course Quick Versatile

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil

Instructions

  1. Scramble eggs and set aside.
  2. Stir-fry rice with vegetables and soy sauce.
  3. Add eggs, mix well, and serve.

Did you know? Dim Sum means "touch the heart" and is traditionally served with tea.

Dim Sum

45 mins Medium
Appetizer Steamed Traditional

Ingredients

  • 1 cup minced pork/shrimp
  • Wonton wrappers
  • Ginger and soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil

Instructions

  1. Fill wrappers with meat mixture.
  2. Steam for 10 minutes.
  3. Serve with soy sauce.

Did you know? Spring rolls are traditionally eaten during Chinese New Year celebrations.

Spring Rolls

30 mins Medium
Appetizer Fried Festive

Ingredients

  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1/2 cup carrots (grated)
  • Spring roll wrappers
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Fill wrappers with vegetables.
  2. Roll tightly and deep fry.
  3. Serve hot with dipping sauce.
